2. **Rank top-10 on Google for тЙе20 long-tail ADHD queries** by month 12 (raised from 15 тАФ more content, more shots).

4. **Publish 4 proprietary research pieces** with original FOCO user data тАФ the GEO citation moat.
5. **Open 3 new audiences** (late-diagnosed women, ADHD couples, the diagnosis-curious) by month 12. Each is currently underserved by competitors.
6. **Build a content moat that takes a competitor 18+ months to copy** тАФ not just volume, but a coherent ADHD-specific voice + system + breadth.

---

## Cadence (sustainable, not bursty)

**Target: 2-3 posts per week. Some weeks 4. Never 0.**

This is what Google's Helpful Content algorithm rewards post-2024. Bursts of 6+ on day 1 then silence look AI-generated. Steady editorial pace looks like a real publisher.

| Week pattern | Output |
|---|---|
| Normal week | 2 posts (Tue + Thu) |
| Heavy week | 3-4 posts (campaign launches, seasonal pushes) |
| Light week | 1 post (review existing, plan ahead) |

**Yearly total: ~120 publishing slots тАФ enough room for 60 new posts + 30 refreshes + 30 buffer days.**

## Risks + how to mitigate

1. **Google updates your traffic to zero.** Diversify тАФ push hard on AI engines (GEO), social, and email list. Don't be a 100% Google site by Q3.
2. **Competitor copies your structure.** Your moat is voice + data. Stay specific, stay personal, ship original research.
3. **Burnout publishing pace.** Better to ship 8/month consistently than 16/month for two months and zero for two. Build buffer of 4-6 finished drafts at all times.
4. **AI engine citation patterns shift.** Re-audit `llms.txt` and schema strategy quarterly. AI engines change what they prefer to cite тАФ don't optimize for one snapshot.
5. **Niche saturation.** ADHD content is exploding. Defense: stay deeper than competitors, ship original research nobody else has.

## What I'd do differently if I had to start over

Honestly, looking at this plan: nothing major. The 6-pillar funnel is sound, the 36-spoke plan is realistic, the original research moat is the right play.

The mistake most ADHD content sites make: they chase keyword volume instead of building authority on a narrow specialty first. We're doing the opposite тАФ owning task initiation specifically, then expanding outward. That's the right call.

The OTHER mistake most content sites make: bulk publishing then silence. We're doing 2-3 posts/week consistent. Also the right call.

If anything I'd skip the lowest-volume spokes (e.g. `russell-barkley-executive-function-model`) and ship 1-2 more comparison posts in P6 instead. Comparison content converts. Educational deep-dives get traffic but rarely conversions. Worth re-evaluating in Q2.
