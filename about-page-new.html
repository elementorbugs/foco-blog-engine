About FOCO

Built for brains that can't start.

You know what to do. You just can't start.

You sat down 47 minutes ago. Your laptop is open. The report is still untouched. You're not lazy — you've been trying.

This isn't a willpower problem. It's a measurable threshold in your prefrontal cortex that hasn't been crossed. The dopamine system that gives neurotypical brains a small reward for "starting" doesn't fire the same way for you. Knowing something matters and feeling motivated to start it are not the same thing.

Every productivity app on the market assumes you can sit down and begin. They give you better calendars, better lists, better timers. None of them solve the gap between intention and action — which is the only gap that actually matters for the ADHD brain.

FOCO targets that gap specifically. Task initiation deficit is the entire product thesis. Everything else is built around it.

🧩

AI Task Breakdown

Tell FOCO a task. It returns 5 tiny steps small enough that your brain agrees to start. No more "clean the kitchen" — instead, "put one dish in the sink."

👥

Body-Doubling Mode

Work alongside the FOCO mascot in a focused session. The perception of being witnessed activates engagement circuits — backed by Russell Barkley's executive function research.

⏱

Focus Timer

Visible countdown ring that externalizes time. For brains with time blindness, watching minutes shrink on a ring is the difference between starting and avoiding.

Why I built this

I'm Adi. I'm a marketer with a tech background. About 1.5 years after my first child, I started forgetting which day it was. I'd open my laptop at 9 AM and find myself still staring at it at 1 PM with nothing done. Tools that worked for my colleagues felt like ridicule.

I don't have a formal ADHD diagnosis. What I have is years of executive function research, a brain that matches the patterns, and the lived knowledge that the gap between "I should start" and "I am starting" is real and physiological — not character.

FOCO is the app I wish had existed. Not another planner. Not a coach. Just the smallest possible bridge over the activation gap, designed by someone who knows where that gap is because she stands in it most mornings.

Common questions

When does FOCO launch?

We're in development with a small private beta. Public launch target is Q3 2026. Joining the launch list gets you the first email when we open up beta seats and the announcement when the app goes live.

Will FOCO work without a formal ADHD diagnosis?

Yes. FOCO is built for anyone whose brain hits an activation wall before tasks — diagnosed, late-diagnosed, self-suspecting, or just exhausted by tools that assume starting is easy. We don't gatekeep the diagnosis.

How is FOCO different from Tiimo, Inflow, or Notion?

Tiimo is a visual planner. Inflow is coaching plus community. Notion is a database. FOCO is a task-initiation tool. We don't try to be your second brain — we try to be the smallest possible bridge between knowing and starting. Different problem, different design.

Is my data sold or trained on?

No. Tasks stay on your device by default. We don't sell to advertisers, share with data brokers, or train models on your private content. The privacy policy spells out exactly what we collect and why — currently, almost nothing.

Who's behind FOCO?

A two-person team. Adi (founder, marketing + product) and a technical co-founder. We're independent and bootstrapped — no VC pressure to maximize engagement at the expense of users.
