What is the best Tiimo alternative for ADHD?

The best Tiimo alternative depends on why you're looking for one. If you want a different visual scheduler, there are several. If you want an app that helps you actually begin the tasks, that's a different category, and that's where FOCO fits.

Tiimo is well-regarded as a visual planning and scheduling app designed for neurodivergent people. Its strength is making your day visible. But seeing your day and starting your day are two different problems, and for many ADHD adults the wall is starting.

So the honest question isn't "what's most like Tiimo," it's "what solves the part Tiimo doesn't."

What is Tiimo good at?

Tiimo earns its reputation. It is built around clear, visual, color-coded timelines that make an abstract day concrete, which genuinely helps with planning and time awareness.

If your main struggle is seeing your schedule, remembering what's next, or building visual structure into your day, Tiimo is a solid choice and we won't pretend otherwise. A good ADHD planning system matters.

The question is what happens after the schedule is built.

Where a visual schedule falls short for some ADHD adults

Here's the pattern many ADHD adults know too well: you build a beautiful, color-coded schedule, and you still don't start the task on it.

That's because a visual planner solves the planning gap, not the initiation gap. Seeing "2pm: write report" on a timeline does not lower the activation energy of actually opening the document. For ADHD brains, that activation energy is the real wall, the same wall behind task paralysis.

If you've tried visual schedulers and still freeze before starting, the issue was never your schedule. It's that scheduling and starting are different skills, and you needed a tool for the second one.

How is FOCO different?

FOCO is built around the moment a scheduler ignores: getting started. Instead of organizing your tasks, it lowers the friction of beginning them.

It does this two ways. First, it breaks a stuck task into one tiny first step, small enough that your brain can't generate enough resistance to refuse it.

Second, it adds body doubling, so you start the task alongside a present companion instead of alone. Working next to someone measurably lowers the friction of beginning, and FOCO builds that in. You can try the free timer and body-doubling room on our ADHD tools page.

Which should you choose?

Choose Tiimo if your honest bottleneck is structure: you lose track of time, forget what's next, and need your day laid out visually. It's good at that.

Choose FOCO if you can plan your day just fine and still can't make yourself begin. If the schedule was never the problem, a better schedule won't fix it. You need something that targets the starting.

FAQ

Is FOCO a good alternative to Tiimo?

It depends on your problem. If you want visual scheduling, Tiimo is built for that. If you plan fine but freeze before starting tasks, FOCO is the better fit because it targets task initiation directly with tiny-step breakdown and body doubling.

What's the main difference between Tiimo and FOCO?

Tiimo helps you see and structure your day. FOCO helps you start the tasks. One solves planning, the other solves initiation, which is why some ADHD adults use both.

Can I use a planner and FOCO together?

Yes, and many people do. Use a visual planner or any planning system for structure, and use FOCO for the moment you need to actually begin. They address different gaps and work well side by side.

Why do I plan my day but still not start anything?

Because planning and starting use different parts of executive function. A schedule lowers confusion about what to do, but not the activation energy of beginning. For ADHD brains, starting is a separate skill that needs its own support.

The Bottom Line

Tiimo is a strong visual scheduler. People look for an alternative when they realize scheduling was never their real bottleneck, starting was.

If that's you, the answer isn't a prettier schedule. It's a tool built for the first step. FOCO breaks the task down and starts it with you, so the wall between knowing and doing finally gets smaller.
