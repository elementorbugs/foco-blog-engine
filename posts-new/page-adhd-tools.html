Which tool should you use right now?

If you cannot start the task in front of you → the 10-minute timer first.

If your head is too full to think clearly → the brain dump method first, then a timer.

If you can think but cannot focus → body doubling, with a timer running.

If you're already working and want to extend → the 30-minute timer.

Why these tools work for ADHD specifically

⏱️

Timers externalize time blindness

ADHD adults underestimate task duration by 30-50%. A visible countdown is the cheapest, most reliable correction available.

📤

Brain dump externalizes working memory

ADHD working memory holds 3-4 items vs the typical 4-7. Offloading open loops onto paper frees the buffer to think.

🤝

Body doubling provides co-regulation

Another presence helps your nervous system regulate when self-regulation is unavailable. Most ADHD adults find it within five minutes.

FAQ

Are these tools really free?

Yes. No signup, no email capture, no premium tier. The tools work in any browser on phone or desktop. The FOCO mobile app is the only paid product.

Do I need to create an account?

No. Click the timer, use it, leave. Nothing is saved to a server — your session stays in your browser.

Why so many timer lengths?

ADHD attention spans vary wildly by task, time of day, medication state, and energy level. A 25-minute Pomodoro is too long when you're foggy and too short when you're flowing. Pick the length that matches the moment.

Is body doubling really effective?

For most ADHD adults, yes — and often dramatically so. The mechanism is co-regulation: your nervous system borrows the calm of another presence. More on body doubling here.

What's the difference between these tools and the FOCO app?

The tools are browser-based, free, and address single moments (focus, dump, double). The app is mobile-first, lives in your pocket, and handles the bigger workflow — task breakdown, voice capture, focus mode, and emotional support via the mascot.

Can I use these on my phone?

Yes. All tools are mobile-responsive. The timer is especially good as a phone tool — leave it running on a tab while you work on something else.
