How FOCO's voice capture works in detail

One tap, speak, done

Open FOCO. Mic button is on the home screen. Tap and hold to speak, release to send. The mic is also accessible via iOS / Android share sheets — you can voice-capture a stuck task from anywhere on your phone.

Transcription happens on-device when possible

iOS and Android both have on-device speech-to-text. FOCO uses it when available — your voice never leaves your phone. For complex or long utterances, the app may fall back to server transcription with explicit permission.

Auto-flows into task breakdown

By default, voice capture treats whatever you said as a stuck task and runs it through breakdown. If you wanted something else (a note, a reminder), you can tap to switch modes after the fact. Default is the most common use case.

You can edit the transcript before processing

If the transcription got something wrong, you see it before FOCO acts on it. Tap to edit, then confirm.

When voice capture helps most

The overwhelm moments

You're frozen. The thought of opening an app and typing feels like its own task. Voice removes that layer of friction.

The walking-around moments

A stuck task occurs to you while you're moving — at the gym, on a walk, between meetings. You don't want to lose the thought but typing is impractical. Voice keeps the thought alive without breaking your motion.

The low-energy moments

Tired, foggy, post-meal slump. Even simple text entry feels disproportionate. Voice keeps FOCO accessible when energy is low.

The hands-busy moments

Holding a baby. Stirring something. Cleaning. Voice captures stuck tasks the moment they arise instead of waiting for "later" (which often becomes "never" with ADHD).

How it pairs with other FOCO features

Voice capture is the input. Everything else is downstream — task breakdown generates the steps, time estimates calibrate each step, focus mode runs the timer, and the mascot stays present while you do the work.

FAQ

Does FOCO record my voice?

No. Voice is transcribed to text in real time, then the audio is discarded. Only the text version is processed and stored. On-device transcription means your voice never leaves your phone in most cases.

What if my accent or speech pattern is hard to transcribe?

iOS and Android transcription has improved dramatically. Most accents and speech patterns work well. You see the transcript before FOCO processes it — you can correct any mistakes before they affect the breakdown.

Can I use voice for things other than task breakdown?

Yes — voice notes, brain dump entries, quick reminders. Default is task breakdown because it's the most common need, but you can switch modes after dictating.

Is voice capture in the free version?

No. Voice capture is a paid mobile app feature. The free web tools at /adhd-tools/ are text-input only.

Does voice work when I'm offline?

On-device transcription works offline (iOS 17+ and recent Android). Task breakdown requires connectivity since it uses AI processing — that step happens when you reconnect.
