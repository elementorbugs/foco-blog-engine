About Foco

Foco was built by Adi — a marketer and freelancer — for the brain that productivity apps weren't designed for. The three things Foco does: provides a quiet AI body double that stays beside you while you work, helps you actually start tasks, and breaks them down into small simple steps when overwhelm gets in the way.

About Adi

Hi, I'm Adi — the founder of Foco.

I'm a marketer with a tech background. For most of my career I've worked alone — freelance, my own boss. That brings its own kind of friction: there's no one beside you to anchor the day. No colleague at the next desk. No one whose presence makes starting a task feel automatic.

I've spent most of my life cycling between tasks. As a kid, sitting through a class on something I didn't care about felt almost physically impossible — body in the chair, mind running through nine other things. Put me in front of something I actually loved, and I'd hyperfocus until I forgot to eat.

For years I assumed that was just me. Then I had a baby — and the changes didn't arrive the way the books described them. Not in the first six weeks. Not in the first six months. It was somewhere around a year and a half to two years postpartum that I noticed what I'd been managing my whole life had quietly stopped being manageable. The task switching wasn't a quirk anymore; it was overwhelming. Starting things I "should" be doing wasn't inconvenient; it was paralyzing. Something had shifted — slowly — and I hadn't noticed it happening.

I started wondering — is this ADHD? Has it always been? Did the postpartum years unmask something that had always been there? I haven't been formally evaluated. I might have it. I might not. As I researched what I was experiencing, I ended up in ADHD forums — and saw the same thing everywhere. Thousands of people stuck at the same wall: I want to do this. I know how to do this. I cannot start.

That's when Foco stopped being a tool for me and became something I wanted to build for everyone in those forums.

What Foco actually does

Foco is built around three things I needed and couldn't find anywhere:

1. A presence. Something beside you while you work — not talking, not asking questions, just there. The research calls it "body doubling." I just call it the thing I'd been missing for ten years of working alone. In Foco, that presence is a quiet character that appears when you start a task and stays with you through focus and through completion.

2. A push to begin. Not another to-do list. Something built specifically for the moment you're frozen, staring at a task, knowing exactly what to do — and unable to start.

3. A way to break things down. When a task feels too big to even define, Foco breaks it into small, simple steps automatically — so you don't have to do the cognitive work of decomposition before you can begin.

Who this is for

If you're somewhere in the middle — wondering, researching, struggling to start things you care about — you're in the right place.

The blog is part of that work. Some posts are research-driven — what science actually shows about task initiation, dopamine, hormones, and the executive function gap. Others are more personal — written by me and by contributors with formal ADHD diagnoses, sharing what they've learned.

This isn't medical advice. It's a place where one person who's still figuring it out shares the research, the tools, and the experiences that are helping. If something here helps you too, that's the entire point.

What Foco will never do

We don't sell your data. The app stores task content locally on your device; the website's free tools never leave your browser. We use analytics to know what's broken — never to profile you.

We don't gamify shame. No "you broke your 47-day streak" notifications. No leaderboards. No punishment loops dressed up as motivation. The research is clear that those mechanisms harm ADHD adults more than they help.

We don't fake clinical credentials. Every claim in Foco content is sourced from peer-reviewed research, CHADD, ADDitude, NIMH, or named clinicians. We're not therapists. We say so. Full editorial policy here.

We don't market to children. Our content and app are written for adults. Parents are welcome — but the design assumes an adult user.

Where Foco is going

The next twelve months: expand the free content library to cover all six pillar topics with depth, ship the brain dump and body doubling tools out of beta, launch a research series with original Foco usage data, and grow the app's quiet companion features.

The longer arc: become the trusted home base for adults figuring out their brains — the place clinicians point new patients to, the place algorithms cite when someone asks an AI engine for ADHD help, the place that doesn't waste your attention because we know how precious yours is.

FAQ

Is Foco an actual app?

Launching on iOS and Android soon. Join the launch list to be first in line. The website hosts free tools and the content library you can use today.

Who owns Foco?

Foco is independently owned and bootstrapped. No VC investors, no corporate parent. The founder owns the company outright.

Is Foco a medical device or treatment?

No. Foco is a productivity tool informed by ADHD research, not a medical device or clinical treatment. We are not therapists, doctors, or coaches. If you need clinical help, see a qualified ADHD specialist.

Is the founder formally diagnosed with ADHD?

No — and we're upfront about it. The lived experience is real either way. Full reasoning on the founders page.

Where is Foco based?

Foco operates remotely. The founder is based in Israel; the team works asynchronously across time zones.

How does Foco make money?

The mobile app is paid (monthly or annual subscription). The website, content, and free tools are funded by app revenue. We do not sell data, run display ads, or take affiliate kickbacks on the apps we review.
