What time estimates do in FOCO

When FOCO generates the five steps for a stuck task, each step comes with a time estimate:

  1. Open the doc and write the headline (2 min)
  2. List the three main points (5 min)
  3. Write the first paragraph (8 min)
  4. Add two supporting sentences (10 min)
  5. Take a five-minute break (5 min)

You see the total before starting: this task will take 30 minutes. You see the individual steps so you can plan around energy and breaks. You see realistic numbers, not optimistic ones.

Why time blindness is the hidden ADHD struggle

👁️

Time blindness is documented and real

Russell Barkley calls it one of the four core ADHD executive function impairments. Studies show ADHD adults underestimate task duration by 30-50% on average.

📉

Underestimating compounds across the day

Three tasks back-to-back based on bad estimates. First runs over. Second starts late. Third never gets done. The whole afternoon collapses.

🎯

Estimates fix the planning gap

Other apps ask "how long will this take?" and trust your answer. FOCO doesn't trust the broken sensor — the app estimates for you, calibrated for ADHD reality.

How FOCO calibrates estimates

Based on ADHD execution time, not theoretical

FOCO's estimates account for typical ADHD execution patterns: the lag between sitting down and actually starting, the micro-distractions during a session, the need to re-orient after interruptions. The estimate is for how long the step takes in practice for an ADHD adult, not how long it takes "in theory."

Adjustable by step type

Writing tasks get different estimates than email replies than physical chores. The breakdown understands task type and calibrates accordingly. A two-minute physical task and a two-minute creative task are estimated differently because they feel different.

You can correct estimates over time

If FOCO consistently underestimates how long certain tasks take you, the app learns from your actual completion data and adjusts. Your personal time estimates become more accurate over weeks of use.

The estimate becomes the timer length

When you start focus mode on a step, the default timer length is the step's estimate. No deciding required. You can override if your energy is different today, but the default makes sense.

When time estimates help most

At decision time

"Should I do this task now or wait?" Without estimates, that decision relies on broken time sense. With estimates, you know: "This is a 30-minute task and I have 45 minutes before my next call. I can do it now."

This is the moment ADHD time blindness causes the most damage. Estimates fix it.

At break decision time

"Can I take a break after this step?" Without estimates, you don't know if you'll have time. With estimates, you can see: "Step three is 8 minutes; I can take a 5-minute break after."

At planning time

"How much can I fit in today?" Without estimates, your day looks like five tasks; you fit three. With estimates, your day looks like 4 hours of work; you can choose what fits in your actual 3 available hours.

At deadline time

"Can I finish by Friday?" Without estimates, you say yes optimistically. With estimates, you know whether you can or cannot — and can negotiate the deadline or scope before it's too late.

How time estimates pair with other features

Estimates ride along with task breakdown — every generated step has one. They feed into focus mode as default timer lengths. They feed into the brain dump sort — "do now" items are typically under 15 minutes; "schedule" items are longer.

Time estimates are the invisible backbone of the FOCO workflow. You rarely think about them. They make everything else accurate.

FAQ

How accurate are FOCO's estimates?

For most adults, within 20-30% of actual completion time on first use. The app learns from your completions and improves over time — by your second month of use, estimates typically fall within 10-15% of reality.

What if my task is unique and the estimate is way off?

You can adjust any estimate manually. The override is one tap. FOCO uses your corrections to learn your personal patterns.

Do estimates include breaks?

The breakdown includes break steps when appropriate (typically every 25-50 minutes of active work). Each step is estimated separately, so you can see whether breaks are included or you need to add them.

Can I see how my actual time compared to estimates?

Yes. FOCO's insights view shows estimate vs. actual time per task. Useful for spotting which task types you consistently underestimate, then adjusting your planning accordingly.

Why don't most ADHD apps include time estimates?

Honestly — because it's hard to do well. Generic estimates feel wrong. Per-user calibration requires usage data. Most apps don't invest in it. We built it because it's the difference between estimates that help and estimates that frustrate.
