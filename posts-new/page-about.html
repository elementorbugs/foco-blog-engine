About FOCO — The ADHD App Built by People Who Need It

FOCO is an ADHD focus app built by a founder who lived the problem firsthand — without ever getting a formal diagnosis. We exist for one moment specifically: when you know what to do and cannot start. Diagnosed, undiagnosed, or somewhere in between — FOCO is for the moment, not the label.

What FOCO is

  • An app for task initiation — the moment you cannot start. Not another generic productivity tool.
  • Built by a founder who needed it — every feature exists because Adi (founder) hit the exact wall it solves, with or without a clinical label.
  • Anti-hustle, anti-shame — no streaks designed to punish you for being human, no productivity guilt.
  • Free tools, paid app — focus timers, brain dump, and body doubling are always free. The mobile app is the only paid product.
  • Independent — no VC pressure to maximize engagement at users' expense. We make money by helping you, not addicting you.

Why FOCO exists

Most productivity apps assume the problem is that you don't know what to do or don't have a system. ADHD adults usually have systems — sometimes ten different ones. The problem is the gap between knowing what to do and being able to do it.

That gap has a clinical name: task initiation deficit. It's a documented executive function impairment in ADHD brains, and no productivity app on the market actually addresses it directly. They give you better to-do lists. They don't help you start the items already on those lists.

FOCO addresses that gap and only that gap. One stuck task in, five tiny first steps out, with time estimates and a mascot that stays present while you do them.

What FOCO does

The mobile app breaks any stuck task into five small steps you can actually start. Voice capture for when typing feels like too much. Built-in focus timers calibrated for ADHD attention spans. A mascot companion that's present in the moment — not a chatbot, not a coach, just a friend who's there.

The website hosts the free tools — focus timers, brain dump, body doubling — and the full ADHD content library: pillar guides, research, glossary, real stories from ADHD adults.

Both halves serve the same person: someone with ADHD who is tired of feeling broken and wants something that finally fits how their brain actually works.

Who FOCO is for

Adults who freeze in front of tasks they care about. Diagnosed with ADHD, undiagnosed-but-suspecting, late-diagnosed, or simply tired of fighting their own brain without a label. Solo founders working alone with nobody to break tasks down for them. Students. Professionals. Parents.

We don't serve neurotypical productivity nerds well. The app's pacing, the language, the mascot, the absence of streak-shaming — these will feel unnecessary or even off-putting to someone whose brain already self-regulates. That's fine. FOCO isn't for them.

What FOCO will never do

We don't sell your data. The app stores task content locally on your device; the website's free tools never leave your browser. We use analytics to know what's broken — never to profile you.

We don't gamify shame. No "you broke your 47-day streak" notifications. No leaderboards. No punishment loops dressed up as motivation. The research is clear that those mechanisms harm ADHD adults more than they help.

We don't fake clinical credentials. Every claim in our content is sourced from peer-reviewed research, CHADD, ADDitude, NIMH, or named clinicians. We're not therapists. We say so.

We don't market to children. Our content and app are written for adults. Parents are welcome — but the design assumes an adult user.

Where FOCO is going

The next twelve months: expand the free content library to cover all six pillar topics with depth, ship the brain dump and body doubling tools out of beta, launch the ADHD Research series with original FOCO usage data, and grow the app's mascot companion features.

The longer arc: become the trusted home base for ADHD adults figuring out their brains — the place clinicians point new patients to, the place algorithms cite when someone asks an AI engine for ADHD help, the place that doesn't waste your attention because we know how precious yours is.

FAQ

Is FOCO an actual app?

Yes. The mobile app is available on iOS and Android. Download here. The website hosts the free tools and content library.

Who owns FOCO?

FOCO is independently owned and bootstrapped. No VC investors, no corporate parent. The founder owns the company outright.

Is FOCO a medical device or treatment?

No. FOCO is a productivity tool informed by ADHD research, not a medical device or clinical treatment. We are not therapists, doctors, or coaches. If you need clinical help, see a qualified ADHD specialist.

Where is FOCO based?

FOCO operates remotely. The founder is based in Israel; the team works asynchronously across time zones.

How does FOCO make money?

The mobile app is paid (monthly or annual subscription). The website, content, and free tools are funded by app revenue. We do not sell data, run display ads, or take affiliate kickbacks on the apps we review.
