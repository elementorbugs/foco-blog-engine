What the mascot does

When you start a task in FOCO, the mascot appears on screen. It is a small animated character — calm, present, not demanding attention. It stays while you work. It is there in focus mode. It changes expression based on what you're doing — focused mode during work, alignment when a task completes, drift when you've been still for a while.

It does not talk at you. It does not interrupt. It does not measure your productivity. It is simply present.

How the FOCO mascot is different from a chatbot

It doesn't talk

Chatbots demand engagement. They ask questions. They suggest things. They make you respond. Each interaction is a cognitive load.

The FOCO mascot says nothing. It is present, the way a friend reading their book at the next desk is present. No performance expected, no responses needed.

It doesn't coach

"Coaching" apps tell you to try harder, push through, be more disciplined. ADHD adults already have those voices in their heads, usually quite loudly.

The FOCO mascot does not coach. It does not encourage. It does not say "you got this!" It is calm and quiet — its presence is the message.

It expresses state, not demands

The mascot has six visual states: presence (calm), alignment (something worked), focus (actively working), drift (you've been still), pause (you stopped), and completion (you finished). The state reflects what's happening, not what the app wants from you.

You can ignore the mascot entirely. Or you can use the state cues — drift state often means it's time for a break; pause state often means the task got too big and needs re-breaking.

It does not perform analytics

The mascot is not measuring you. The state changes are visual cues for your benefit, not data the app is collecting to score your productivity. There is no productivity score. There is no streak.

When the mascot helps most

Solo work hours

You're alone in your space. The room is quiet. The work is harder than it should be because there's nobody to absorb the loneliness. The mascot won't make you not alone — but it will be present in a way that softens the edge.

Focus sessions

You start a 50-minute focus block on a hard task. The mascot is on the corner of the screen. You're not performing for it. You just feel less isolated than if the screen were blank.

The afternoon crash hour

Most ADHD adults have a predictable afternoon energy crash. The mascot's presence during that hour can be the difference between pushing through one more task and giving up. Not because it motivates — because it stays.

When you start a task you've been avoiding

The moment you finally tap to start a long-avoided task is the moment most ADHD adults bail. The mascot appearing at that moment provides a soft witness — someone (something) is there with you for this start, which makes it harder to abandon ten seconds later.

FAQ

Can I turn the mascot off?

Yes. Settings include a minimal-UI mode that hides the mascot. Most users keep it on, but the option is there.

Does the mascot use the camera?

No. The mascot is animated client-side. The app does not access your camera unless you explicitly use the (optional) face-presence feature for advanced body doubling. Default is animation only.

Is the mascot AI?

The mascot's animations are pre-built, not AI-generated. The state transitions follow rules based on your activity (idle time, task progress, focus mode state). No AI is "watching" you or making real-time decisions.

Why a mascot and not just a human on video?

Video body doubling works but requires scheduling and a willing partner. The mascot is always available, no coordination needed. For users who want video body doubling with real people, that's on the roadmap as a separate feature (shared focus sessions).

Will there be other mascot characters?

Probably eventually. For now there's one FOCO mascot — it represents what the brand stands for. Variants are on the roadmap but not the priority.
