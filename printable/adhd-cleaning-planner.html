The 5 ADHD Cleaning Rules

Most cleaning planners assume you can just start and keep going. These rules are built for a brain that can't, and that's the point.

1. One room. Close the doors.

Pick a single room and ignore the rest of the house. The whole house is not the task today. This room is.

2. Set a timer. Stop when it rings.

10 or 15 minutes. Cleaning ends when the timer ends, even mid-task. This kills the "if I start I'll lose the whole day" fear.

3. Body double, don't go solo.

Starting is easier with company. Clean alongside a friend, a video, or a FOCO body-doubling session so the activation energy drops.

4. Done beats perfect.

Every checklist here has a "good enough" line. Hit that and you are finished. Deep-clean energy is a bonus, never the bar.

5. Missed a day? Skip it.

No catch-up, no doubling up, no guilt pages. You start again today. Today is the only day that matters.

Calm beats frantic. Small and steady wins.

Start Here When You're Overwhelmed

When the mess is so big your brain shuts down, ignore the rest of this planner. Do only this.

You do not have to finish. You do not have to do it well. You only have to start the timer. That is the entire win.

The 15-Minute Reset

  • Set a timer for 15 minutes. When it rings, you are allowed to stop and be done.
  • Trash lap. Grab a bag, walk one loop, throw away only obvious garbage. Don't sort, just toss.
  • Dish gather. Carry every cup and plate to the sink. Stacking counts. Washing is optional.
  • One surface. Pick the single worst flat surface and clear just that one.
  • Stop and look. Find the one spot you cleared. Say "that counts." Because it does.
Still stuck before step one? That's task initiation, not laziness. A FOCO session starts the timer with you so you're not doing it alone.

The Daily 10-Minute Reset

Not deep cleaning. Just enough to stop the mess from snowballing. Ten minutes, same six moves, every day you can.

  • Make the bed. Pull the covers up. That's it, no hospital corners.
  • Clear one surface. Pick the worst counter or table and reset it.
  • Dishes home. Into the dishwasher or one neat sink stack.
  • Trash + cups. One lap, collect rubbish and stray mugs.
  • 5-thing pickup. Put exactly five out-of-place things where they live.
  • Wipe the sink you used. Ten seconds. Future-you says thanks.

Pair it with the same daily cue, coffee brewing, kettle boiling, so it rides an existing habit instead of willpower.

Kitchen & Bathroom

Each step is small on purpose. Tick what you can. Hit the "good enough" line and walk away proud.

Kitchen
  • Dishes into dishwasher or one sink stack
  • Clear and wipe one counter
  • Wipe the stovetop
  • Trash out, fresh bag in
  • Sweep the main walkway only
  • Wipe the sink and faucet
Good enough: counters clear and the sink is empty.
Bathroom
  • Trash out, including old products
  • Wipe sink, faucet, and counter
  • Swish the toilet bowl, wipe the seat
  • Spray and wipe the mirror
  • Swap in fresh towels
  • Shake out or sweep the floor
Good enough: sink and toilet are done.

Pick the room with the lowest activation cost today, not the dirtiest. Momentum is the goal. The hard room is easier once you're already moving.

Bedroom & Living Areas

Same idea: shrink it down. The floordrobe and the cup collection are not moral failures, they're just the next two ticks.

Bedroom
  • Make the bed
  • Clothes: hamper or hang, clear the floor
  • Clear and wipe the nightstand
  • Cups, plates, and trash out
  • Wipe one surface (dresser top)
  • Quick floor pass for the visible bits
Good enough: bed made and floor walkable.
Living areas
  • Cups and plates back to the kitchen
  • Cushions and blankets reset
  • 10-thing pickup (put them where they live)
  • Clear one surface or coffee table
  • Quick floor pass for visible crumbs
  • Tidy the cable and remote chaos
Good enough: surfaces clear and floor walkable.

The Monthly Deep-Clean Rotation

These are the tasks that pile up into a scary mountain. So don't batch them. Pick one per week and the mountain never forms.

Wipe inside the microwave + one fridge shelf
Change the bed sheets
Dust shelves, screens, and sills
Clean one set of windows or mirrors
Bathroom deep clean: tub and shower
Vacuum under the couch cushions
Wipe baseboards in one room
Declutter one drawer or shelf
Mop the kitchen + bathroom floors
Wash one set of towels + bath mat

Write the date next to one when you do it. Ten tasks, one a week, means the "deep clean" is always already handled in the background.
