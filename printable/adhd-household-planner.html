The 5 ADHD Home Rules

Most household planners assume you can hold the whole system in your head and keep it running. These rules are built for a brain that can't, and that is the point.

1. One zone a day.

Not the whole house. One area, today. The rest of the home is not the task.

2. Set a timer. Stop when it rings.

10 or 15 minutes. This kills the "if I start I'll lose the whole day" fear.

3. Get it out of your head.

Your brain is for ideas, not storage. Dump the mental load on the pages built for it.

4. A bad day has a bare minimum.

On a hard day, a full win is tiny, and that still counts. There's a whole card for it.

5. Missed it? Skip it.

No catch-up, no doubling up, no guilt. You start again today. Today is the only day that matters.

Calm beats frantic. Small and steady wins.

Start Here When the House Feels Impossible

When the whole house feels like too much and your brain shuts down, ignore the rest of this planner. Do only this.

You do not have to fix the house. You do not have to do it well. You only have to start the timer. That is the entire win.

The 15-Minute Home Reset

  • Set a timer for 15 minutes. When it rings, you are allowed to stop and be done.
  • Trash lap. One bag, one loop, obvious garbage only. Don't sort, just toss.
  • Dishes to the sink. Carry every cup and plate over. Stacking counts. Washing is optional.
  • One surface. Pick the single worst flat surface and clear just that one.
  • One tiny admin thing. Send one text, or pay one bill. Too much today? Skip it, that's allowed.
Still stuck before step one? That's task initiation, not laziness. A FOCO session starts the timer with you so you're not doing it alone.

The Doom Pile Reset

The chair, the floordrobe, the everything-table. You don't clean it, you sort it. One pile, 15 minutes, four buckets.

The pile I'm tackling today:

🗑 Trash

Obvious garbage. Don't agonize over it, just bin it.

➡ Belongs elsewhere

One basket. Everything from another room goes in, deal with it later.

✓ Belongs here

Put it where it actually lives, right now, while it's in your hand.

⏸ Decide later

One box, lid on. Write a date to revisit so it's not forever.

Set 15 minutes. Sort, don't clean. When it rings, whatever's left goes in the Decide-later box and you are done.

The stuff we avoid most

Life Admin & Errands

Admin is the hardest ADHD task: boring, low-dopamine, easy to avoid until it becomes a crisis. Shrink each one to a single next step and do it in a focus session.

Calls & messages

e.g., dentist, doctor, that callback, the school, a reply you owe

Bills, forms & errands

e.g., electric bill, a return, renew a prescription, a form, cancel a subscription

Park it (not now, but don't lose it)

Admin is the perfect FOCO task: set 10 minutes, make the one call, stop. The dread is almost always bigger than the task.
